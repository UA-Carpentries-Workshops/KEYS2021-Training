This resource is maintained by the Software Carpentry organization (SWC). Part of the The Carpentries, organization, Software Carpentry aims to help researchers get their work done in less time and with less pain by teaching them basic research computing skills. SWC workshops are hands-on and cover basic concepts and tools, including program design, version control, data management, and task automation. Participants will be encouraged to help one another and to apply what they have learned to their own research problems.

For more information on what SWC teaches and why, please see our paper "Best Practices for Scientific Computing".
